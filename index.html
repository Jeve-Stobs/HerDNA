DNA & Heredity Timeline

AFRICAN AMERICAN'S WHO CONTRIBUTED [NOT IN CHRONOLOGICAL ORDER]

1955

Herman was an American physicist and chemist. He is best known for his contributions to the Alpha Helix Protein Structure. Without his understanding of biochemical roles of proteins, we would be unable to create the vaccines that we have today.

1940

William Cardozo was a physician and pediatrician. his research and contribution to Sickle Cell Anemia was crucial in helping discover that the diease was mainly hereditary and not spread through the air.

1980

Marie was a biochemist who was also the first Black woman to earn a PhD in Chemistry. She contributed heavily to protein synthesis and the chemistry of histones.

1951

James Bowman was an American physician and specialist in pathology, haematology and genetics. His discoveries helped him understand favism and G6PD and its deficiencies.

1988

Rick Kittles is an american biologist who specializes in human genetics. His contributions to heredity helped him discover how African ancestry was tracked. His company, African Ancestry Inc. helps Africans trace their ancestral roots.

1743

Benjamin Banneker was a distinguished mathematician, astronomer, & surveyor. He created intricate clocks that told time to "faultless precision". He was considered a mechanical genius with a magic touch.

1850

Thomas Jennings was the first African American to recieve a patent. He earned his patent in 1821. The patent was for a modern dry cleaning process which was revolutionary for its time..

1978

Georgia M. Dunston is a professor of human immunogenetics at Howard University. She has explored genetic variations within human MHC genes and antigens and their relationship with disease in African Americans.

1967

Jewel Plummer Cobb was an American biologist and cancer researcher. She mainly worked on melanin and skin damage. The hardest forms of cancer. She was an advocate for representation of women and students in university.

2020

In July 2020, Samuel Casey contributed to the Linux kernal. An open source operating system that allows contributions to bug fixes and feature additions. This specifc contribution was to a securtiy bug that had plagued Raspberry Pi's.
